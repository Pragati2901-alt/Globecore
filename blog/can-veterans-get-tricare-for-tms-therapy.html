Can Veterans Get TRICARE for TMS Therapy? (Complete Coverage Guide)

07/04/2026 By Kelly Lewis-Arthur

Mental health conditions such as depression and PTSD affect a large number of veterans. While traditional treatments like antidepressants and psychotherapy are effective for many, some individuals continue to struggle with persistent symptoms. This has led to increased interest in TMS therapy as a non-invasive, drug-free alternative.

A key concern for veterans is whether this advanced treatment is covered under TRICARE. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of TRICARE coverage for TMS therapy, eligibility requirements, and how to access care.

What Is TMS Therapy?

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is an FDA-approved treatment primarily used for major depressive disorder. It uses magnetic pulses to stimulate specific areas of the brain associated with mood regulation. Unlike medications, TMS does not circulate through the bloodstream, making it ideal for those who experience side effects from antidepressants.

Does TRICARE Cover TMS Therapy?

Yes, TRICARE provides coverage for TMS therapy under specific conditions. It is typically approved when:

  • The patient is diagnosed with Major Depressive Disorder (MDD).
  • The condition is classified as treatment-resistant (standard medications haven't worked).
  • A licensed provider confirms medical necessity.
  • Prior authorization is obtained.

Eligibility for TRICARE Coverage

TRICARE benefits extend to a wide group, including:

  • Active-duty service members.
  • Military retirees and veterans.
  • National Guard and Reserve members.
  • Eligible dependents and spouses.

TRICARE Requirements for Approval

Approval is based on strict medical guidelines. Veterans must usually demonstrate that they have tried at least two antidepressant medications from different classes without sufficient results. They may also need to show a history of psychotherapy.

Note for PTSD Patients: TRICARE does not typically cover TMS for PTSD alone. However, if a veteran has both PTSD and MDD, coverage can often be approved under the depression diagnosis.

Step-by-Step Recovery Process

  1. Evaluation: Consult a TRICARE-authorized psychiatrist for an assessment.
  2. Documentation: Gather records of previous treatments and medications.
  3. Authorization: Your provider submits clinical documentation to TRICARE.
  4. Treatment: Once approved, therapy can begin at an authorized facility like GlobeCoRe.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost with TRICARE?

For most active-duty members, there is no out-of-pocket cost. Veterans and retirees may have a small copayment depending on their specific plan (Prime vs. Select).

Is TMS therapy safe?

Yes, TMS is FDA-approved and non-invasive. Most patients report only mild scalp discomfort during the first few sessions. There are no systemic side effects like weight gain or nausea.
